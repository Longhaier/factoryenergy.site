# Predictive Maintenance for Facility Equipment: Eliminating Failures Before They Happen

"Equipment broke again!" This is probably the last thing every factory floor wants to hear. Traditional equipment maintenance mode is "repair after failure", but this passive maintenance not only affects production but may also lead to serious safety incidents. In recent years, a new maintenance concept - predictive maintenance - is being adopted by more and more enterprises. Today let's discuss this topic in depth.

## Dilemmas of Traditional Maintenance Modes

### 1. Reactive Maintenance
Repair after equipment fails:
- Loss already occurred, unrecoverable
- Emergency repair costs are high
- May cause safety incidents
- Affects production plans and delivery

### 2. Scheduled Preventive Maintenance
Maintain on fixed schedules:
- May cause over-maintenance, wasting resources
- Maintenance cycle is difficult to precise
- Some failures cannot be avoided by scheduled maintenance

### 3. Condition-Based Maintenance
Decide maintenance timing based on equipment condition:
- More accurate than scheduled maintenance
- Still requires human judgment
- Low data utilization rate

## What is Predictive Maintenance?

Predictive Maintenance (PdM) is an advanced maintenance mode that uses sensors to collect equipment data, analyzes data to predict failures, and performs maintenance before failures occur.

Core idea: **Don't wait for equipment to break before repairing, predict and repair in advance.**

## Technology Foundation of Predictive Maintenance

### 1. Sensor Technology

Modern sensors can monitor:
- **Vibration sensors**: Detect bearing wear, imbalance
- **Temperature sensors**: Detect overheating, cooling system anomalies
- **Oil sensors**: Detect wear particles, moisture content
- **Electrical sensors**: Detect current, voltage anomalies
- **Acoustic sensors**: Detect leaks, discharge

### 2. Data Analysis Technology

- **Statistical models**: Trend analysis based on historical data
- **Machine learning**: Learn failure patterns from large amounts of data
- **Deep learning**: Handle complex nonlinear relationships
- **Digital twins**: Build virtual mirrors of equipment

## Application Scenarios for Facility Equipment

### 1. Air Compressor Units

Monitoring parameters:
- Vibration (bearing condition)
- Temperature (cooling system)
- Oil quality
- Exhaust volume (performance degradation)
- Energy efficiency ratio (comprehensive evaluation)

Prediction content:
- Bearing wear degree
- Cooler blockage
- Oil-gas separator blockage
- Performance degradation trends

### 2. HVAC Systems

Monitoring parameters:
- Refrigerant pressure
- Evaporator/condenser temperature difference
- Vibration (compressor condition)
- Energy efficiency ratio
- Indoor temperature/humidity deviation
