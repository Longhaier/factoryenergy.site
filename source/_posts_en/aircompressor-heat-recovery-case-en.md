# Reducing Energy Consumption by 30%: Real Case Study of Compressed Air System Heat Recovery in Semiconductor Factories

In semiconductor manufacturing, compressed air systems are the real "energy hogs". A 200kW air compressor running 24 hours can cost hundreds of thousands of yuan in electricity bills every month. But few people know that 80% of this electrical energy is converted into heat and wasted. Today, let's discuss how to turn "waste heat" into "treasure".

## How Much Heat Does Compressor Generate?

Let's calculate: for a 200kW screw compressor:
- Input power: 200kW
- Effective work: about 40kW (20%)
- Heat loss: about 160kW (80%)

This 160kW of heat recovery equals to:
- Annual electricity savings: about 500,000 yuan
- Hot water supply for 1,000 people
- Winter heating for the factory building

## Three Main Ways of Heat Recovery

### 1. Hot Water Recovery
Install heat exchangers in the compressor's lubrication system to transfer heat to softened water:
- Water output: 3-5 m³/h
- Water temperature: 60-85°C
- Applicable scenarios: factory bathrooms, boiler preheating, canteen water

### 2. Air Preheating
Use recovered heat to preheat fresh air entering the factory:
- Can reduce HVAC load by 20-30%
- Especially suitable for northern regions in winter
- ROI period: 2-3 years

### 3. Generator Drive
The most efficient method - use recovered steam to power a small generator:
- Power generation efficiency: 15-20%
- Can provide 10-15% of factory's electricity
- But requires higher initial investment

## Case Study: IC Packaging Factory

A semiconductor packaging factory implemented heat recovery transformation:

**Before:**
- Compressor power: 3×200kW
- Annual electricity cost: 5.2 million yuan
- Heat emissions: equivalent to 360kW

**Transformation:**
- Installed 2 plate heat exchangers
- Built 50m³ hot water storage tank
- Added circulating pump control system

**After:**
- Annual hot water: about 30,000 tons
- Replaced gas boiler: 400,000 yuan savings annually
- Annual CO2 reduction: about 500 tons
- Investment payback period: 2.5 years

## Key Points and Precautions

1. **Temperature Matching is Critical**
Compressor oil temperature is usually 80-90°C. Choose appropriate recovery temperature range based on actual use.

2. **Water Quality Management**
Recovered hot water hardness must not be too high to avoid scale affecting heat exchange efficiency.

3. **Smart Control System**
Use PLC control system to automatically adjust recovery based on hot water demand.

4. **Safety Measures**
High-temperature pipelines need insulation, hot water storage tanks need safety valves and temperature alarms.

## Conclusion

Compressed air system heat recovery is a "highly profitable" energy-saving measure. Mature technology, high ROI - if your factory has compressed air systems, consider having a professional company do an assessment.
