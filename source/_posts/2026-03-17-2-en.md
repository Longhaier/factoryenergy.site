# Facility Shift Log Template: Share Your Experience

## How Important Are Shift Logs?

Let me tell you a story.

One weekend evening in 2018, the factory's AC main unit threw an error. The night shift guy simply wrote "AC malfunctioned" and went home. When maintenance arrived Monday morning, the compressor was fried—if they'd handled it that night, replacing a solenoid valve would have fixed it. Instead, we spent 80,000 RMB on a new compressor.

**Shift logs aren't just paperwork. They're insurance.**

## The Template We Use

Here's our shift log template—simple but effective:

```
[Facility Shift Log]
Date: 2026-03-17
Shift: Day/Night

[Equipment Status]
1. HVAC: Normal / Abnormal (Details: ____)
2. Power System: Normal / Abnormal (Details: ____)
3. Water Supply: Normal / Abnormal (Details: ____)
4. Fire Safety: Normal / Abnormal (Details: ____)

[Inspection Records]
- 08:00 Electrical room: Temp____ Humidity____
- 10:00 HVAC room: Unit ____ running
- 14:00 Pump room: Outlet pressure____MPa
- 16:00 Terminal inspection: Zone temps____℃

[Exception Records]
- Time: ____
- Equipment: ____
- Phenomenon: ____
- Action taken: ____
- Result: ____

[Handover Notes]
1. ____
2. ____

[Remarks]
____
```

## Key Point: How to Write Good Exception Records

This is what I want to emphasize most. Many logs say "equipment abnormal" and then nothing.

**A proper exception record needs:**
1. **Time** - When it was discovered
2. **Phenomenon** - Specific details (alarm codes, parameter issues, strange noises)
3. **Initial judgment** - Possible cause
4. **Action taken** - What was done
5. **Follow-up recommendation** - Who needs to follow up

## Go Digital

Many factories now use digital shift log systems. Some we've tried:

- **DingTalk/Feishu logs** - Mobile-friendly, supports templates
- **SCADA systems** - Directly connected to equipment, automatic logging
- **Specialized facility software** - Such as "FacilityDog"

Benefits of going digital: traceable, statistical, never lost.

## Bottom Line

Shift logs—if you're going to write them, do it properly.

Not to pass inspections, but to **save your butt when it matters.**
